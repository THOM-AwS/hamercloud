## Nathan Whiston's Family (Martha's father)

Nathan Whiston — shoemaker, lived in Clun, "enterprising and industrious", reared 7 children:

1. **Martha Whiston** (eldest) — born at Clun, m. John Hamar, d. Broxton Mills aged 84
2. **Aaron Whiston** — oldest son, lived at Knighton, married, 2 children
   - Aaron (son) — draper in London, d. ~1867
   - A daughter — married into malster family, lived in Knighton
3. **(A son)** — married a widow with 7 children
4. **Richard Whiston** — shoemaker, d. in hospital ~1866 aged 90, 1 daughter who m. ? Jones, went to America
5. **(A son)** — lived at old Homestead, 3 children (2 sons died), daughter m. William Price (farmer, 3 miles from Knighton)
6. **Nathaniel Whiston** — died in America, miller
   - Charles Whiston (born England)
   - Nathan Whiston (born England)
   - Anne Whiston (born England)
   - John Whiston (born Ebensburg, Pennsylvania)
   - Benton Whiston (born Ebensburg, Pennsylvania)
7. **Jane Whiston** — m. Martin Luther, d. ~1870 aged 82, 4 children: Martha, Mary, Hannah, John

## Notable Facts

- **Jack Hamar** (son of Arthur, grandson of AF-9 William) — shot down at Dunkirk, WWI/WWII. Peggy (his mother) received Royal Medal.
- **Carlton Smith** (grandson of AD-4 Richard via Adelaid) — killed flying over France, WWI.
- **Ernest Hamar** (son of AF-7 Edward) — India Police, served in WWI.
- **Alfred Hamar** (AD-41) — ran 1,100 acre farm at Bicton with 1,100 Clun sheep and 267 cattle.
- **Edward Hamar** (son of AD-4 Richard) — 94 years old when visited in 1937.
- The American Hamers were concentrated in **Johnstown and Ebensburg, Pennsylvania** and **St. Joseph and Cameron, Missouri**.
- O. Stuart Hamer and Harry M. Hamer were the family historians who compiled and verified this document.

## Occupations Across the Family

Grocers, drapers, millers, shoemakers, tailors, farmers, carpenters, a tipstaff in Court of Exchequer, India Police, soap merchants (London and Paris), wine/liquor dealers, bankers (married into family).
